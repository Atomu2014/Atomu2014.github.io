Deep Recommender System for Huawei App Market
Program Leader, Advisors: Prof. Weinan Zhang, Prof. Yong Yu.
Mar. 2017 - Mar. 2018, Huawei Co. Ltd
This is a joint program between APEX Lab (SJTU) and Noah's Ark Lab (Huawei Co. Ltd) with over CNY 1 million fundings. In this program, I serve as the program leader, supervised by Prof. Weinan Zhang and Prof. Yong Yu.
Current state-of-the-art recommendation models can be categorized into matrix factorization (MF) based, tree based, and deep neural network (DNN) based. Through deep and thorough analysis, we find a coupled gradient issue of MF based models, and an insensitive gradient issue of DNN based models. In fact, DNN may not be a perfect approximator as MF does in learning feature interactions, which is known to be critical for recommendation. On the other hand, MF-based models have limited model capacity compared with DNNs. Thus, we propose a novel deep learning framework for recommendation, which utilizes a feature extractor to learn interactive patterns, and a DNN classifier to enhance model capacity, called Product-based Neural Networks (PNNs). We propose several types of feature extractors, including kernel product and network-in-network, making it flexible and expressive. The proposed kernel product method defeats the winning solution (libFFM) in Criteo Display Advertising Challenge, proving it effective and efficient. PNN shows state-of-the-art performance in offline evaluation, and achieves over 35% CTR improvement in online A/B test. Corresponding works are accepted by ACM Transactions on Information System, and are regarded as a high-value patent by Huawei Patent Committee. An open source project of the model gets 200+ stars on Github.

Transfer Learning for Named Entity Recognition
Advisors: Prof. Weinan Zhang, Prof. Yong Yu.
June. 2017 - Nov. 2017, APEX Lab
In healthcare domain, Named Entity Recognition (NER) is one of the most important methods to process unstructured medical records, and data insufficiency is one of the biggest challenges. A solution to data insufficiency is transfer learning. In this task, we study cross-specialty transfer learning for NER. Currently, the most popular NER model is LSTM + CRF architecture, where a (bi-)LSTM model is used to learn text representations from the input, and a condition random filed (CRF) model is used to map text representations to the desired entity sequences. Most of the previous works on NER transfer mainly focus on introducing domain-invariant constraints to LSTM. However, in real world, the conditional probability (entity conditioned on text) may not be identical among different specialties, making these models not applicable to such cases. To solve this problem, we propose a double (LSTM and CRF) transfer learning framework for NER transfer. Besides, we introduce a label-aware prior to LSTM, and an L2 constraint on CRF parameters. We prove the L2 distance in parameter space is indeed equivalent to the KL-divergence in CRF output distributions. The work is accepted by NAACL with oral (6.73%) representation.

Text-driven Graph Embedding with Pairs Sampling
Advisors: Prof. Weinan Zhang, Prof. Yong Yu.
Mar. 2018 - Aug. 2018, APEX Lab
Random Walk (RW) based models are the most applied Graph Embedding models, which simulate random walks on graphs and generate node sequences to represent graph structures, and finally use skipgram model to train node embeddings. In this work, we analyze the objective of RW and propose a new explanation from the perspective of neighborhood joint probability. According to the new objective, we propose an efficient sampling policy which reduces more than 99.9% training pairs compared with RW. Besides, we study the scenario of large-scale graphs with rich texts, and propose an inductive graph embedding method to make full use of textual information.

Wasserstein Distance Guided Representation Learning for Domain Adaptation
Advisors: Prof. Weinan Zhang, Prof. Yong Yu.
Mar. 2017 - Aug. 2017, APEX Lab
Domain adaptation studies the case of transfer learning when source domain and target domain only differ in input distributions. Most of the works in domain adaptation focus on learning domain-invariant representations, i.e., learning projection functions which map source/target domain input data to the same hidden space. One popular solution to domain adaptation is by adding a maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) constraint to the learned representations, which is based on the technology called Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space. Another solution is by learning a domain critic. In this work, we follow the idea of domain critic, and propose an adversarial representation learning approach, which uses Wasserstein distance to measure the domain discrepancy and plays a minimax game between the feature extractor and the domain critic. Besides, we provide a generalization bound guarantee and a gradient analysis. The proposed method shows good performance on several real-world datasets, and the visualization results confirm the effectiveness of our method.
